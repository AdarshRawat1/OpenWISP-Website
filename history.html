OpenWISP History

The "Provinciawifi" project was born in October 2008 with the aim to create the largest open and free of charge Wi-Fi network ever realized in Italy, by installing 500 access points in the main public squares of the Municipalities of the provincial territory of Rome.
In this context, a research project conducted by the CASPUR consortium found its perfect application. This project proposes an innovative methodology for the distribution of networks (more precisely Virtual LANs used for Wi-Fi connectivity) between geographically distant sites.

The methodology used makes it possible to host public connectivity services on non-dedicated network infrastructures (e.g.: private xDSL) that for technical and legal reasons could not be used as such.
This is not the sole advantage that the architecture developed by CASPUR ensures to the Provinciawifi project: embracing the resource-sharing philosophy – typical of "open" projects – anyone can contribute to the network expansion simply by hosting an access point.
The benefits of the agreement between the Province of Rome and CASPUR were not late to arrive: just after two months (i.e. December 2008) there were more than 50 hot-spots dislocated on the entire territory of the Province. Such a feat would be practically impossible for the common architectures used for Wi-Fi networking, given the very rigid Italian regulatory laws in this matter.
In the course of 2009, a fundamental year for Provinciawifi network growth, the expansion requirements in terms of number of access points and increasing amount of service users, made it necessary to develop appropriate software tools to improve the simplicity and speed of Provinciawifi network management.

The software tools and the architectures are now being released to the public free of charge with open-source licenses. By allowing the community to download, use and contribute to the first open-source software tools for Wireless Internet Service Providers management we want once again to honor the "open" philosophy that made Provinciawifi great.

Who is using openWISP?

Many Italian Public Administrations are using openWISP software for their Wi-Fi network.

  • Provincia di Roma - Provinciawifi
  • Consorzio Romawireless - Romawireless
  • Comune di Genova - FreeWiFiGenova
  • Comune di Torino - FreeTorinoWiFi
  • Comune di Lamezia Terme - LameziaFreeWiFi
